# Shree Saraswati Secondary School — Website

## Product Purpose
A public-facing website for a rural Nepali secondary school in Gulmi, Lumbini Province. Serves students, parents, and the local community with information about academics (General ECD-12 + Technical Computer Engineering), admissions, notices, events, teacher profiles, and photo galleries. The site is the school's primary digital presence north of Kathmandu valley.

## Users
- **Parents in Satyawati Rural Municipality** — checking admission dates, notices, and teacher credentials. Literacy varies; information must be scannable and visually clear.
- **Students (Grade 5-12)** — browsing gallery, events, exam portal. Want energy and pride in their school.
- **Community members / visitors** — looking for location, contact info, and a sense of the school's reputation.
- **School administration** (via admin panel) — updating notices, slides, teachers, gallery.

## Brand
- **Register**: brand (marketing / community-facing)
- **Tone**: warm, dignified, grounded. Not corporate. Not overly casual. A school that takes itself seriously without being stiff.
- **Anti-references**: Nepali government school templates (crowded, low-contrast, clip-art); SaaS-cream (overly polished with no soul); international private school luxury (Nepal's rural schools don't pretend to be Eton)
- **Strategic principles**:
  - The site should feel like the school itself — modest, solid, cared for
  - Color comes from the Nepali landscape: green hills, blue sky, red accents
  - Typography should handle both English and Devanagari gracefully
  - Photography is the hero; the UI steps back and frames the people and place
  - Every section earns its place — no filler
- **Physical scene**: A parent in Johang village checks the school website on their phone after dinner. The light is warm indoor light, maybe a kerosene lamp or low-watt LED. They need to find admission dates quickly. A student looks at the gallery on a shared family phone, feeling proud seeing their friends in the photos.
- **Theme**: Light. The warmth of welcoming families matters more than a dark-mode "tool" aesthetic. The scene demands approachability, not drama.
- **Color strategy**: Committed — one saturated color (a warm green-teal from the Nepali landscape) carries 30-60% of the surface. Deep navy for structure. Gold/amber as a small accent for warmth.

## Voice
- "We" not "the institution"
- Short sentences. Plain English. No jargon
- Nepali phrases appear naturally (motto, greetings)
- Headlines can be declarative: "Admission Open," "Our Teachers," "Notice Board"
